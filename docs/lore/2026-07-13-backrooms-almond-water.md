# Backrooms Almond Water ("Object 1")
### Reference for the almond water asset — silhouette, behavior, canon posture, IP hazards

**Status:** Research reference. Not a design decision record.

## 0. The one thing that matters

Almond water is the internet's most famous case of **lore drift**. It entered the mythos in 2019 as a *cyanide warning* and, by mass misreading, became the setting's *healing potion*.

The earliest known mention (r/backrooms, 2019, written in-character as a chat log, attributed to u/JackalofSpades): the water coolers are drinkable, but *"if you smell almonds, don't drink it."* Bitter almond → hydrogen cyanide → you are already dead. Readers parsed the hazard as a product name. Within about two years it was **Object 1** — the most abundant, most beloved consumable in the Backrooms, dispensed from vending machines.

So there are three assets you could ship, and two of them are opposites:

| Version | What it is | Who expects it |
|---|---|---|
| **Post-drift** | Heal / sanity restore consumable | Every player who has heard of the Backrooms |
| **Pre-drift** | Cyanide in a friendly bottle. A lethal mimic. | Nobody — which is the point |
| **The inversion** | It does what the population *believes* it does | The source material itself (§6) |

**The inversion is the only version with teeth,** and it is not a subversion I invented — the Backrooms Wiki did it to itself. See §6.

**Prestige-tier adaptations reject this item outright.** Kane Parsons' series and the A24 film (29 May 2026) keep the Backrooms and drop levels, entities, and almond water. The purist split (r/TrueBackrooms) predates them. If the project's thesis is foundation-grade canon vs. slop, **almond water is Exhibit A for the slop side.** Ship it straight and you inherit that. Ship the inversion and you are commenting on it.

## 2. Silhouette — pick one

| Source | Container | Liquid | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| ● Fandom lineage + most games | Glass/plastic bottle with a branded label | Clear | Flavor described as sweet almond + vanilla + rose water. **Label is a real trademark — see §5.** |
| ● Escape the Backrooms | Yellow aluminum can, ~13.5 × 5.2 cm (a 250 ml energy can) | — | The most-copied silhouette in the fandom. Which is why I'd avoid it. |
| ○ Wikidot (current rewrite) | Dented metal **thermos**, ~1 ft, holds 2–4 cups, scuffed paint | Clear | Color-coded body: grey / green / red / blue (§4) |
| ○ Miraheze | Small bottle | **Yellowish**, slightly thicker than water | In-fiction, the yellow tint made early wanderers assume it was poisoned. |

**Recommendation:** the **thermos** reads as *scavenged* — dents and worn paint do a decade of environmental storytelling for free, and it dodges both the trademark (§5) and the most-cloned mesh in the genre. Reserve the branded bottle as a rare/older variant if you want the recognition beat.

## 4. Wiki lore — shared core, then the forks

**Shared across all three wikis (●):** abundant on nearly every level; sweet almond taste; nutritionally impossible; origin unknown and unobserved; valuable enough to serve as a **barter unit / de facto currency**; harmful to some entities.

| | Wikidot (main) | Fandom | Miraheze |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Form** | Colored metal thermoses | Victoria's Kitchen–branded bottles | Yellowish bottles |
| **Nutrition** | ~600 kcal/bottle; 3/day sustains an adult | Minor; cures minor illness | ~400 kcal; regenerative |
| **Variants** | **Grey** (pure nutrition, tames some entities), **green** (caffeine, energy-drink sweet, addictive), **red** (bitter, antibiotic — the *only* treatment for The Disease), **blue** (melatonin, sleep, blocks external influence on the psyche) | Rare colored bottles: muscle/stamina | — |
| **Danger** | On certain levels it still causes **cyanide-like poisoning**. Standing advice: *when in doubt, boil the water.* Never drink a half-empty bottle. | **Expires** into mold; expired = poison. Cashew Water may just be expired almond water. | Some supplies too filthy to drink |
| **Supply** | Unexplained; ancient texts say it simply appeared one day | **Spawns only when unobserved** | Teleported in from "The Stockpile"; a trade group monopolizes and inflates it |

**Steal-worthy mechanics from this table:**
- **The observation rule** (Fandom): it only materializes where nobody is watching. A respawn justification that is *also* creepy. Free.
- **Boil before drinking** (Wikidot): converts a pickup into a verb, a fire, and a decision under pressure.
- **The half-empty bottle taboo** (Wikidot): a half-empty bottle means somebody else drank from it and didn't finish. Instant environmental storytelling, zero VO.
- **Color-coded variants** (Wikidot): four bottles, four consumable classes, one mesh + material swap. Cheapest content-per-asset ratio in the doc.

## 5. The real-world tether — IP hazard, read this

Fandom canon says almond water is manufactured in the Frontrooms by **Victoria's Kitchen**. That is a **real company and a real product**: a French family-recipe beverage (water, sugar, almond flavor, citric acid; vanilla-and-rose profile), pitched on *Shark Tank* S6 (no deal), acquired by Hispanica International in 2017, now effectively dead — fans still petition for its return specifically because of the Backrooms. The wiki's flavor description ("vanilla and rose water") is lifted straight off the real can's ingredients.

**Do not put that label on the asset.** It is live trade dress belonging to someone else, and nearly every fan model copies it. Design an original label. Keep "vanilla and rose" as flavor text — a flavor note isn't protectable, a logo is.

## 6. Object 100 — the metafiction, and the best design brief available

Wikidot's `object-100`, *"Super Almond Water"* (CutTheBirch), is the wiki writing about its own drift:

An in-universe researcher digs up the original 2019 "smells like almonds" quote, concludes almond water **used to be cyanide**, and theorizes the Backrooms rewrites itself to match collective belief. To test it, he deliberately seeds a rumor that almond water has become an all-curing panacea — "Super Almond Water." The rumor takes. The M.E.G. tells wanderers to drink it daily. Then the people who drink it start dying, tasting blood and cashews, feeling a burning in the skull. The page closes on the note that the Backrooms began as a single paragraph and became folklore too large for any community to control.

**That is the asset's story, and it is already canon-shaped:**

> **Belief is the mechanic. The item does what the population thinks it does.**

Concrete hooks that fall out of it for free:
- The item's stat block **changes** as in-world belief changes (NPC rumors, posted notices, player-visible "everyone says this heals").
- Two identical bottles, different histories. The one nobody has heard of is inert; the famous one is potent — and the *poisonous* one is potent for the same reason.
- The "smells like almonds" line becomes a **diegetic warning nobody heeds anymore**, because the name of the thing is now the warning. That is the whole joke, playable.

**Real chemistry backing the pre-drift version** (use for flavor text, it's accurate): bitter almonds contain amygdalin, which releases hydrogen cyanide; HCN's bitter-almond odor is the classic poisoning tell — and **20–40% of people are genetically unable to smell it at all**. A warning half your party cannot perceive is a mechanic, not a trivia note.
