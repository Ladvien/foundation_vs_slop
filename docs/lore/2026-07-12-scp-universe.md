# SCP Foundation Mythos: A Multiversal Design Bible for Game Developers

## TL;DR
- SCP has **no official canon** — authors freely contradict each other, and the "SCP-001" origin slot is deliberately a set of mutually exclusive proposals. For a "tight but not religious" multiversal game, treat a thin layer of consensus mechanics (object classes, Humes/reality-bending, amnestics, the Veil, the major GoIs) as load-bearing, and treat cosmology/origins/conceptual-tier material as freely inventable.
- The multiverse is already deeply systematized in canon: **Ways** (ritual portals), the **Wanderers' Library** (interdimensional hub), **SCP-7005 "Lampeter"** ("over 4000 known universes, with many more suspected to exist," with an explicit universe-designation scheme), the **Department of Multi-Universal Affairs** (Hub/Branch/Floater taxonomy), and the **Temporal Anomalies Department (RCT-Δt)**. These map cleanly onto game systems.
- The para-science stack (**Humes/Kant counters/Scranton Reality Anchors**, **EVE/thaumaturgy**, **Akiva/divinity**, **amnestics/mnestics**, **memetics/antimemetics**) is the richest vein of "translate lore into meters and resources" material and is broadly consensus.

## Key Findings

1. **The multiverse is over-determined, not under-determined.** There are at least four independent, well-developed canonical frameworks for cross-dimensional travel and cataloging. A game can pick one as its spine and treat the others as flavor without breaking canon.
2. **"Difficulty of containment, not danger"** is the single most-scrutinized piece of fan knowledge. Official guidance states it plainly: object classes measure containment difficulty — "A world-ending bomb that never goes off is Safe; a teleporting kitten is Keter." Getting this wrong is the #1 amateur tell.
3. **The Anomaly Classification System (ACS)** — the Disruption/Risk overlay (Dark/Vlam/Keneq/Ekhi/Amida; Notice/Caution/Warning/Danger/Critical) — is real, official, and used on newer articles, but is **contested and not universally adopted**; many respected authors and whole branches ignore it. Don't treat it as mandatory lore.
4. **Three distinct "power systems"** exist and fans police the distinctions: thaumaturgy (learnable, EVE-fueled, ruled magic), psionics (mind/signal powers, technologically measurable), and reality-bending (innate Hume-based reality alteration). Your psionic party role should be mechanically distinct from a "wizard."
5. **CC BY-SA 3.0 permits commercial games** — the license explicitly says you may "Sell: You can sell the remixes you make based on the SCP Foundation" — but imposes attribution and share-alike. The share-alike clause is the real design constraint: derivative SCP content must itself be released under CC BY-SA 3.0.

## Details

### 1. Multiversal / Dimensional Lore (highest priority)

**The Pataphysics Department & narrative anomalies (Site-87, Sloth's Pit, Wisconsin).** Pataphysics in SCP means "the metaphysics of metaphysics" — the idea that reality is fiction and can be manipulated as narrative. Research Site-87 (disguised as "S & C Plastics") observes the Shangri-La/Nexus town of Sloth's Pit, a town "powered by pataphysics." Key concepts: the **noosphere** (the conceptual/idea-layer of reality), **Navidson-class** narrative anomalies (entities that turn reality into fiction and trap you "until the story ends"), and the notion of "the Author." Site-87 also headquarters the Department of Multi-Universal Affairs and Nexological Studies. The associated 001 proposal is **I.H. Pickman's Proposal ("Story of Your Life")**. Design note: pataphysics is the canon's built-in justification for a game to be self-aware, break the fourth wall, or treat save/load and respawn as diegetic — but it is tonally divisive; use sparingly.

**Department of Multi-Universal Affairs ("Multi-U").** Uses an **H-B-F classification**: **Hub** universes (important "trunk" realities), **Branch** universes (differ from their parent by some change, from trivial to an XK-event), and **Floater** universes (no apparent connection to anything). Operational doctrine from the in-canon "Multi-U 101" orientation: observe don't interact ("you're the SCP in this scenario"), pack light, and if something dangerous could bleed through, "close the door behind you." Travel hardware includes the **MUTA (Multi-Universal Transit Array)**, e.g., the MUTA-NT ("Nexus Type"). Design gold: this is a ready-made mission structure — expeditions to catalogued alternate universes with a "prime directive" tension.

**Department of Interdimensional Logistics & SCP-7005 "Lampeter."** Lampeter is a decaying multidimensional transport network (trains, cars, planes, hot-air balloons, the Mongol yam system, plus pocket-universe transit hubs) that moves between timelines/universes/realities. Per the article (author Tufto): "Its full extent, both in this universe (A001 'Prime') and others, comprises over 4000 known universes, with many more suspected to exist." It uses an explicit **universe-designation scheme** — our universe is **A001 "Prime"**; others carry codes like **Z999 "Halogen"** (Lampeter's origin universe) and **L453 "Harald's Hole."** It was founded in the 13th century in Universe Z999 by the Lampeter family and run by the **Lampeter Non-Euclidean Shipping Company (L-NESC)** until it went bankrupt in 2021, after which the Foundation stepped in — "Foundation control currently extends only to 832 distinct stations." The associated threat, the **Neon God**, is a city-concept deity that originated in Z999 and drove the Lampeters to build the network to flee; the L-NESC's collapse threatens "the numerous Z-Class scenarios this would precipitate," with full Foundation control "expected to be complete in 2035 2050 2070" (the struck-through dates signal an escalating, likely-unwinnable timeline). Design gold: the A001/Z999 coding gives you a diegetic universe-ID system for a save-file/level-select UI, and the "decaying network manned by the mad and the lonely" premise is a ready-made hub-world tone.

**Temporal Anomalies Department / RCT-Δt.** Research & Containment Team Delta-t, hubbed at Temporal Site-01, holds a unique **"Δ" (Delta) clearance/recruitment protocol**. Its cornerstone tech is the **Xyank/Anastasakos Constant Temporal Sink (XACTS)** — a device (room-temperature relativistic accelerator + radio transmitter) for temporal containment, notable for being "retroactively distributed as far back as the 1890s." Directors: Dr. Thaddeus Xyank (born 2415, temporally displaced ~500 years into the past) and Dr. Athena Anastasakos. Concepts: retrocausality, stable Grandfather Paradoxes, Causal Isolation Protocols (CIPs), counter-historical anomalies. Design gold: time-travel mechanics with a canonical containment-device vocabulary.

**The Wanderers' Library, the Serpent's Hand, and Ways.** The **Wanderers' Library** (designated Nx-01; called KTE-7909-Alexandria by the GOC) is a near-endless extradimensional library cataloging nearly all information in the multiverse, run by the Serpent and staffed by Librarians. It is accessed only via **Ways** — ritual portals (e.g., the "New York Knock": chicken blood over a manhole) that connect locations, dimensions, and universes. Time flows differently inside. The **Serpent's Hand** (GoI Alpha-019) is a decentralized anti-containment resistance HQ'd in the Library, calling the Foundation "the Jailors" and the GOC "the Bookburners." Ways are surrounded by "**Ontic Bleed**" radiation. The Library is a sister-site with a "wonder" tone rather than horror. Design gold: Ways are the canonical, low-tech, ritual-flavored alternative to tech-based dimensional travel — good for a magic-user traversal system.

**Key multiversal SCPs to reference:**

| SCP | Name | What it is | Game use |
|---|---|---|---|
| SCP-2000 | Deus Ex Machina | Underground facility under Yellowstone that rebuilds humanity after a K-Class event using cloning + amnestics; enforces ~20 Hm stable reality | The canonical "reset button"/respawn justification |
| SCP-1730 | "What Happened to Site-13?" | Dimensionally-warped derelict site; multiversal horror setpiece | Dungeon/level template |
| SCP-5000 | "Why?" | Foundation exterminates humanity; erupts Yellowstone (destroying SCP-2000); alternate-Foundation intrigue | High-stakes alt-timeline plot |
| SCP-3812 | A Voice Behind Me | Reality-bender ascending an infinite "narrative stack"; each higher narrative sees the lower as fiction | Conceptual-tier antagonist (handle with care) |
| SCP-4755 / SCP-3125 | conceptual-tier | "Conceptual hierarchy" tiers; antimemetic god-entities | Cosmology backdrop only |
| SCP-6000 | "The Serpent, the Moon, and the Library" | A Way that turns Earth into a section of the Library | Multiversal endgame event |

**On conceptual-tier / "narrative layers" material:** This is the most contested and least load-bearing material in the entire mythos. Fan power-scaling communities (VS Battles, etc.) obsess over it, but on the main wiki it is author-specific (djkaktus, Placeholder McD, S. Andrew Swann) and mutually contradictory. **Recommendation: treat it as optional cosmological set-dressing, never as a system.** A multiversal game can safely ignore or lightly gesture at "the Author"/narrative-layer material.

**Anomalous cities & pocket dimensions:**
- **Three Portlands** ("TriPorts") — largest North American paranormal enclave, connecting Portland OR/ME/England; anart + paratech hub; discovered by the Foundation in 1970; US law enforced there by the UIU under the "Hoover Mandate."
- **Backdoor SoHo** — pocket-universe anartist haven off NYC's SoHo, guarded by a Doorman ("Charlie").
- **Alagadda** — extradimensional city (SCP-2264-B) ruled by the **Hanged King**; modeled on Carcosa/The King in Yellow.
- **Esterberg, New Amsterdam, Eurtec (paratech), 8837 London** (asteroid colony) — additional enclaves in the Third Law canon.
- **Nexus** classification: locations rated Briar (barely anomalous) → Dunwich/Crossroads (fully anomalous); some are "Free Ports" where the Veil doesn't apply.

### 2. The Paratech / Parascience Stack (model these as systems)

**Humes, Kant counters, Scranton Reality Anchors.** The **Hume (Hm)** is the unit of "amount of reality" in an area; baseline consensus reality is 1 Hm (some sources use ~20 Hm for a stabilized site). Measured with a **Kant counter** (contains micro-portals to a 0 Hm and a 100 Hm pocket dimension for calibration). **Reality benders ("Type Greens"/ontokineticists)** have personal Hume levels exceeding their surroundings; the Foundation grades them roughly Class 1–5 (Class 5 ~860–900 self-Hm can trigger a CK-class event; an unofficial "Class 6" = deity-tier). **Scranton Reality Anchors (SRAs)** stabilize/raise ambient Humes to suppress reality-bending — they don't "suppress" bending so much as out-bend it, siphoning reality from dead/dying universes. "Banalization": crushing a weak anomaly with dense ambient reality to force it to obey physics. Design gold: **Hume level = a stability/reality meter**; SRAs = deployable stabilizer objectives; reality benders = boss-tier enemies whose power scales with a Hume differential.

**EVE / Thaumaturgy.** **EVE (Elan-Vital Energy)** is the energy emitted by living/sapient things that powers **thaumaturgy** (magic-as-applied-science). Thaumaturgy follows predictable rules: the **Laws of Similarity and Contagion** and three principles — "Like Produces Like," "The Part Affects the Whole," "Observation Changes Reality." Practitioners = **thaumaturges / "Type Blues" / sorcerers**. Crucially, thaumaturgy is *learnable* by anyone with skill (not innate). "Aspect Radiation" = EVE concentrated enough to cause reality-warping effects. This is the axis for the **Sarkic** (carnomancy) and **Daevite** (blood-thaumaturgy) traditions.

**Akiva radiation / Tactical Theology.** The **Akiva** is the unit of divine grace/faith (measured by an **Akiva Counter / thaumometer**, quantified in centiAkiva). High Akiva facilitates **theurgy** (prophecy, miraculous healing, exorcism of Tartarean/spectral entities). Belief generates it — places of worship, and even secular belief systems (the "American civil religion" gives Washington D.C. high Akiva). **Crucially, SRAs cannot affect Akiva** — divinity is a separate axis from reality-bending. Design gold: a second, faith-based "power axis" orthogonal to Humes; anti-divine "unholy relics" and Akiva charges as tactical items. The **Horizon Initiative** is the faction that weaponizes this axis.

**The four measurable "para-quantities" (resource/meter candidates):**

| Quantity | Measures | Instrument | Suppressed by | Game meter idea |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hume (Hm) | Amount of reality | Kant counter | Scranton Reality Anchor | Reality/stability bar |
| EVE | Life-energy powering magic | (thaumometer) | — | Mana/spell resource |
| Akiva | Divine grace/faith | Akiva counter | unholy relics (not SRAs) | Faith/holy resource |
| (Aspect Radiation) | Weaponizable EVE flux | — | — | "Overload" state |

**Amnestics & mnestics.** Amnestics = memory-erasing agents, graded by function (the updated canon guide replaces the old A/B/C *strength* model with functional classes):

| Class | Function |
|---|---|
| A | General retrograde (last 5–6 hrs) |
| B | Regressive retrograde (incremental, back ~24 hrs at 75 mg) |
| C | Targeted retrograde (surgical, specific memories) |
| D | Progressive retrograde (early memories) |
| E | Ennui (induces complacency toward the anomalous) |
| F | Fugue (erases/rebuilds identity) |
| G | Gaslighting (induces doubt in memory authenticity) |
| H | Anterograde (prevents new memory formation) |
| W–Z | **Mnestics** (reverse amnestics; used by the Antimemetics Division) |

Note the old "Class A = strongest" model still appears in older articles — this is a known internal inconsistency; the newer functional model is the current guide. **Class Z mnestic** = total inability to forget, fatal by seizure within hours. Design gold: amnestics are the diegetic explanation for NPC memory-wipes, D-class turnover, and the Veil; mnestics are how a player character "sees" antimemes.

**Memetics / antimemetics / cognitohazards.** **Cognitohazards** harm you by perception; **infohazards** harm you by information; **memetic** effects self-propagate as ideas; **antimemes** are ideas that erase themselves from memory (you forget them the moment you stop perceiving them). The **Antimemetics Division** handles the latter — a fan-favorite subseries (qntm's "There Is No Antimemetics Division"). The **Berryman-Langford Memetic Kill Agent (MKA)** is a visual meme causing cardiac arrest and is the standard gate on SCP-001; the hub warns verbatim: "ANY NON-AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ACCESSING THIS FILE WILL BE IMMEDIATELY TERMINATED THROUGH BERRYMAN-LANGFORD MEMETIC KILL AGENT. SCROLLING DOWN WITHOUT PROPER MEMETIC INOCULATION WILL RESULT IN IMMEDIATE CARDIAC ARREST FOLLOWED BY DEATH." (The name references David Langford's 1988 story "BLIT" — Berryman Logical Image Technique.) Design gold: antimemes are a superb stealth/perception mechanic (enemies you forget when you look away; a mnestic-drug "sight" meter).

**Paratech inventory:** **.aic** = Artificially Intelligent Conscript (Foundation AIs: 8-Ball, Glacon, Alexandra, Mnemosyne); **Scranton devices/boxes**; **Kant counters**; **XACTS** time sinks; **eigenweapons/paraweapons**; **Anderson Anchors** and **Lang-Scranton Stabilizers** (variant reality anchors).

### 3. Threat & Event Classification Systems

**Object (Containment) Classes** — measure containment difficulty, not danger:

| Class | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Safe | Easy to contain (often: inert unless activated) |
| Euclid | Unpredictable / insufficiently understood (most sapient anomalies) |
| Keter | Very hard to contain; elaborate/ongoing procedures |
| Thaumiel | Used *by* the Foundation to contain other anomalies; existence known to few |
| Apollyon | Uncontainable; expected to cause/precede a K-Class scenario |
| Archon | Could be contained but deliberately isn't (containment would do more harm) |
| Neutralized | Destroyed/defunct |
| Explained | Reclassified as mundane/mainstream science |
| Pending | Not yet classified |
| Decommissioned | Removed (mostly a legacy meta term) |

Official guidance is explicit: "Object classes are for containment difficulty, not danger. A world-ending bomb that never goes off is Safe; a teleporting kitten is Keter." Keter "does not mean the SCP is dangerous, just that it is simply very difficult or costly to contain." The **"Locked Box Test"** is the informal heuristic: imagine the object in a locked box and ask what happens if left alone.

**Esoteric / secondary classes:** dozens exist (Cernunnos, Gödel, Hiemal, Tiamat, Ticonderoga, "Anomalous," etc.); most are one-offs. Only a handful see real use. Post-ACS, articles may carry a Primary + Secondary (Esoteric) containment class.

**K-Class scenarios (end-of-world taxonomy):** The consensus-standard, frequently-used ones:

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **XK** | End-of-the-world (broadest; 100+ articles) |
| **CK** | Reality restructuring (deity/reality-bender/retrocausal) |
| **ZK** | Fundamental destruction of reality/existence |
| **SK** | Dominance shift (another species/civilization supplants humanity) |
| **AK** | Large-scale alteration of human thought |
| **LK** | Humanity itself is altered |
| **NK** | Grey-goo / self-replication runaway |
| **HK** | Deity suppression/apotheosis |
| **BK / EK / MK** | Consciousness/meaning-loss variants |
| **VK, YK, QK, RK, WK, GH** | Physics apocalypses, mass extinction, greenhouse, etc. (niche/inconsistent) |

**Caveat:** Many K-class letters are used inconsistently or as one-offs; XK, CK, ZK, SK, AK are the safe, recognizable ones. The wiki's own "A Comprehensive List of K-Class Scenarios" documents the inconsistency openly.

**The Anomaly Classification System (ACS)** adds two axes to the object class:
- **Disruption Class** (threat to normalcy/the Veil; named for degrees of light): **Dark → Vlam → Keneq → Ekhi → Amida**
- **Risk Class** (danger to an individual): **Notice → Caution → Warning → Danger → Critical**

**Adoption reality:** ACS is official and appears on many newer/high-effort articles, but adoption is **partial and contested** — the Russian branch uses its own S7-APCS; the French branch drops ACS in translation for its own risk-rating; and many authors reject it as raising the barrier to entry. **For a game: ACS is a legitimate, richer HUD option, but do not present it as universal canon.**

**Other designations:** GOC uses its own parallel taxonomy — **KTE (Known Threat Entity)**, **PTE (Potential Threat Entity)**, **LTE (Lifted/cleared Threat Entity)**, **UTE (Undiscovered Threat Entity)**.

**Party-role-relevant faction notes:**
- **Xenobiologist → Sarkicism.** The Sarkic hub frames flesh-craft as anomalous applied genetics disguised as sorcery: "It is the right of the Sarkite to guide and cultivate organic matter," and "Organic manipulation has allowed certain Sarkites to achieve anomalous states of being, transcending the physical limitations of baseline humans." Karcists are biologically immortal, craft chimeric flesh-thralls (Halkost), and revere disease. Proto-Sarkic (bloodline-based, insular, apotheosis only through Ion) vs Neo-Sarkic (self-serving, exploits the rituals) are best treated as distinct religions. This is your richest vein for anomalous-biology mechanics.
- **Psionic → Fifthism / antimemetics.** Fifthist power is memetic/antimemetic (contagious idea-infection, perception-warping) — a flavor cousin to psionics distinct from EVE-magic. Their deity (SCP-3125) is an antimeme. In canon, psionic threats are handled by MTFs such as **Tau-5 ("Samsara")** and **Psi-** designated units.

### 5. The Veil / Normalcy & World-State

The **Veil (Masquerade)** is the maintained policy of hiding the anomalous from the public. Normalcy is treated as a *quantity to be maintained* — via mass amnestic administration, the **Disinformation Bureau**, and cover-front organizations. When it fails, you get the **Broken Masquerade** canon (below): a post-exposure world where the Foundation operates openly. The **anomalous community / paraworld** persists behind the Veil: Three Portlands and Backdoor SoHo (anart scenes, paratech black markets, anomalous currencies), Free Ports where the Veil doesn't apply, and enclaves for parahumans and sapient non-humans.

### 6. History & Deep Lore

- **The Occult Wars**, esp. the **Seventh Occult War** (overlapping WWII), fought among the Allied occultists, the Nazi **Thule Society / Obskurakorps (Ahnenerbe)**, and Japan's **IJAMEA**. The GOC was founded from the war's survivors.
- **Foundation origins:** the **Administrator** (unnamed founder), precursor orgs like **HMFSCP** (British Empire — which coined the term "site" and colour-coded rather than numbered them), consolidated via a late-19th/early-20th-century **Convention on Preternatural Phenomena** (the "Forbidden City Convention" in the Broken Masquerade canon, led by O5-1 "The Visionary," ex-HMFSCP).
- **Apex mythos entities:** the **Scarlet King** (an eldritch anti-creation deity, SCP-001 candidate, tied to SCP-231 and SCP-2317 "the Black Moon"); the **Hanged King** (of Alagadda); the **Brothers Death**; **Yaldabaoth** (primordial flesh-god, tied to Sarkicism); **Mekhane** (the Broken God). All are author-specific and non-canonical across the whole wiki — good raw material, no single "true" version.

### 7. SCP-001 Proposals — "What is the Foundation really?"

There is no true 001; the slot holds many mutually exclusive proposals, each implying a different worldview. Access is gated in-fiction by the Berryman-Langford MKA. Notable proposals and their implied cosmology:

| Proposal | Title | Worldview implied |
|---|---|---|
| Dr. Clef's | The Gate Guardian | Foundation guards humanity from divine/Edenic forces |
| Dr. Bright's | The Factory | Anomalies are manufactured; industrial horror |
| Dr. Gears' | The Prototype | Foundation grew from a single "prototype" anomaly |
| S. Andrew Swann's | The Database | **Metafictional**: the Foundation is a narrative; authors/readers are "SCP-001" — directly relevant to a multiversal/meta game |
| S.D. Locke's | When Day Breaks | Apollyon solar apocalypse |
| I.H. Pickman's | Story of Your Life | Pataphysics/narrative |
| The Scarlet King / The Black Moon | — | Eldritch-doom origin |
| ROUNDERHOUSE's, McDoctorate's, etc. | — | Various |

A multiversal game inevitably brushes origin/meta questions; the **Swann "Database" / author-layer** and **Pickman/pataphysics** proposals are the natural touchstones, but committing to any one is optional. The mythos itself models "many contradictory origins" as a feature (GENERAL NOTICE 001-Alpha: fabricated files hide the "true" ones; maybe all false, maybe all true).

### 8. Canons

There is **no single canon**. The wiki is a shared setting where any author may contradict any other; "canons" are opt-in continuity clusters documented on the Canon Hub. One-line summaries and multiverse-friendliness:

| Canon | What it's for | Multiverse-friendly? |
|---|---|---|
| **Broken Masquerade** | The Veil has fallen; Foundation operates publicly | Yes (post-exposure world) |
| **Third Law** | Urban-fantasy paraworld; enclaves (TriPorts, Backdoor SoHo, New Amsterdam) | Yes (enclaves/pocket dims) |
| **On Guard 43** | Site-43 (Canada); memetics & anomalous-waste focus | Moderate |
| **Ad Astra Per Aspera** | Anomalous space exploration | Yes (space, not multiverse) |
| **Unfounded** | The Foundation never existed; GOC takes its place | Alt-history |
| **Competitive Eschatology** | Rival apocalypses / end-of-world contest | Yes |
| **Resurrection** | Post-catastrophe rebuild arc (amnestics deep-dive) | Moderate |
| **The Trashfire** | Comedic/self-parody (Dr. Wettle) | No |
| **Doomsday** | The 2018 apocalypse contest hub | Yes |
| **Cool War** | Cold-War-era anomalous espionage | No |
| **Void's Song** | Character-driven arc | — |
| **From 120's Archives / S & C Plastics / Those Twisted Pines** | Site-specific character canons | Site-87 = pataphysics/multiverse |

**What "no single canon" means for a "tight but not religious" dev:** you are *expected* to pick and choose. Fans will not object to internal invention; they *will* object to misusing shared vocabulary (calling Keter "the most dangerous class," conflating thaumaturgy with reality-bending, treating ACS as gospel, or getting the GOC's kill-first doctrine backwards). Faithfulness = respecting the *grammar* of the setting, not memorizing any one continuity.

### 9. Practical Game-Dev Considerations

**Licensing (CC BY-SA 3.0 — not legal advice):** The entire SCP wiki is CC BY-SA 3.0. This *permits commercial games* — the Licensing Guide says explicitly, "Sell: You can sell the remixes you make based on the SCP Foundation. However, keep in mind that you probably will not get rich off of them, because under Share-Alike, anyone can freely copy, use, or download your stuff, and you will have no legal recourse." Two obligations:
1. **Attribution** — name the work, the author (screen-name is fine, in quotes), and link the source. General concepts (the Foundation, a GoI) need only a general wiki attribution; specific articles need specific per-article attribution.
2. **Share-Alike** — your derived SCP content must itself be released under CC BY-SA 3.0. The wiki's sample release statement explicitly covers games; GPLv3 is also listed as compatible (but forces public source release).

Precedent: **SCP – Containment Breach** (open-source, CC BY-SA 3.0), **SCP: Secret Laboratory**, and **SCP: Containment Breach Multiplayer** all ship CC BY-SA 3.0 attribution notices. The **share-alike is viral for the SCP-derived content specifically** — this is the real constraint for a commercial studio. The boundary between SCP-derived *expression* (must be CC BY-SA) and your proprietary engine code plus wholly original assets needs careful handling; get counsel before locking your art/code architecture. Individual authors of specific SCPs you feature appreciate (but do not legally require) a courtesy heads-up; some famous SCPs carry author sensitivities about how their signature "cold open" is reused.

**What existing SCP games got right/wrong:** Containment Breach nailed atmosphere and the D-class-in-a-breach premise but is a narrow slice (one site, classic SCPs 173/106/096/079). Fans complain when games (a) over-rely on the same five "greatest hits" SCPs, (b) flatten object classes into a danger scale, (c) ignore the Veil/amnestic logic, and (d) treat the Foundation as unambiguously heroic (canon is morally grey — D-class ethics, the Ethics Committee, GOC atrocities).

**Load-bearing vs safely inventable:**

| Load-bearing (get right) | Safely inventable |
|---|---|
| Object classes = containment difficulty | New SCPs / new sites |
| The Veil & amnestics logic | Specific universe designations |
| Humes / reality-bending / SRAs basics | Exact cosmology / origin (001) |
| Thaumaturgy ≠ psionics ≠ reality-bending | New GoIs or GoI sub-factions |
| GOC destroys, CI terrorizes, Serpent's Hand liberates | Narrative-layer / conceptual tiers |
| D-class are expendable test subjects; morally grey Foundation | New Mobile Task Forces |
| "Secure, Contain, Protect" mission | Your party's specific characters |

**Common amateur tells:** "Keter = most dangerous"; a single omniscient heroic Foundation; SCP-173 as the mascot/first thing shown; conflating amnestic classes' strengths; magic and reality-bending used interchangeably; ACS treated as mandatory; ignoring that most SCPs are boring/benign (the mundane-anomaly texture is part of the charm).

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Lock the spine (pre-production).** Choose ONE multiversal framework as your mechanical backbone. Recommendation: a **SCP-7005/Multi-U hybrid** — use the A001/Z999-style universe-ID scheme for your level/world select, the Hub/Branch/Floater taxonomy for world-generation rules, and **Ways** as the in-world traversal verb for magic-flavored travel. This is the densest, most game-ready canon material and is internally consistent. Benchmark to change course: if playtesters find the universe-ID scheme opaque, fall back to named "Hub" worlds only.

**Stage 2 — Build the meters.** Implement the para-quantities as literal systems: **Hume level = a reality/stability meter** (low Humes near reality-benders and Ways; SRAs as deployable stabilizers), **EVE = the thaumaturge/mana resource**, **Akiva = a separate faith resource** (for theology-flavored content), and **antimemes = a perception/memory mechanic** gated by mnestic items. Keep these orthogonal — that orthogonality *is* the canon.

**Stage 3 — Differentiate the four party roles against canon systems:**
- **Combat/weapons specialist** → Agent/MTF operative; eigenweapons/paraweapons, SRA deployment, Scranton devices.
- **Researcher/scientist** → drives the Kant-counter/Hume readouts, amnestic administration, containment-class assessment (the "Locked Box Test" as a gameplay minigame).
- **Psionic** → mind/signal powers (telepathy/TK/precognition), measurable via a "Psiophone"; explicitly NOT a thaumaturge. Fifthism/antimemetics is the natural flavor cousin.
- **Xenobiologist** → the Sarkicism/carnomancy axis (anomalous biology, flesh-crafting, chimeric creatures) and cataloguing alien biology across Floater universes.

**Stage 4 — Faction and threat systems.** Use the GoI table as a faction-relationship matrix (GOC hostile-to-anomalies, CI hostile-to-Foundation, Serpent's Hand liberationist, MC&D mercantile). Use **object class = containment-difficulty** for a capture/containment loop and **K-class scenarios** as escalating fail-states (XK/CK/ZK as the recognizable tiers). Offer ACS Disruption/Risk as an *optional* advanced HUD, flagged in-world as "new classification system."

**Stage 5 — Licensing hygiene (do this early, get counsel).** Adopt the wiki's sample CC BY-SA 3.0 release statement; build an in-game attribution screen listing every SCP/tale/GoI used with author screen-name and URL. Get a lawyer to opine specifically on the share-alike boundary between SCP-derived expression (must be CC BY-SA) and your proprietary engine/original assets before committing art/code architecture. Benchmark to escalate: any time a designer wants to use a *specific* named SCP's unique expression (not just the general setting), route it through the attribution list and consider an author courtesy-contact.

## Caveats

- **No single canon** is the foundational fact; every "fact" above is contradicted somewhere on the wiki. I have flagged consensus vs author-specific throughout.
- **Source-quality flags:** Primary lore here rests on official scp-wiki.wikidot.com hub/guide pages (object classes, ACS guide, K-class list, amnestics guide, Akiva/Hume guides, Multi-U, SCP-7005, RCT-Δt, Serpent's Hand, GoI, licensing, SCP-001). Where I relied on fan wikis (Fandom, Miraheze, TV Tropes, Villains/Heroes wikis) for connective mythos — especially Sarkic/Broken God/Fifthist deep lore, apex-entity relationships, and the Factory's multiversality — those are community syntheses of multiple stories, not always single-source canon, and should be verified against the specific article before shipping.
- **Conceptual-tier / narrative-layer material (SCP-3812/4755/3125, "the Author")** is the least stable and most fan-power-scaled part of the mythos; treat as set-dressing only.
- **ACS adoption** is genuinely contested; do not present it as universal.
- **Licensing statements here are informational, not legal advice**; the share-alike interaction with proprietary game code warrants professional counsel.
- Numbers cited (Hume baselines, universe counts, amnestic dosages, membership figures) vary between articles; I've given the most commonly cited values and flagged known inconsistencies.
